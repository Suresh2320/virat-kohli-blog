Virat Kohli Biography

Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket. He was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India. Known for his aggressive playing style, consistency, and strong leadership qualities, he has inspired millions of cricket fans around the world.

Early Life

Virat Kohli showed interest in cricket from a very young age. He joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998, where he received professional training. His dedication and hard work helped him progress quickly through junior-level cricket.

Domestic and International Career

Virat Kohli gained national attention after captaining India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. He made his international debut for India in August 2008. Over the years, he became a key player in all formats of the game.

Kohli is known for his excellent batting technique, fitness, and ability to perform under pressure. He has scored thousands of runs across Test, One Day International (ODI), and T20 formats.

Captaincy

Virat Kohli served as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats for several years. Under his leadership, India achieved significant success, especially in Test cricket, becoming one of the top-ranked teams in the world.

Achievements

  • One of the fastest players to score 10,000 runs in ODIs
  • Multiple ICC awards for best cricketer
  • Former captain of the Indian national team
  • One of the most followed athletes in the world

Personal Life

Virat Kohli is married to Indian actress Anushka Sharma. He is known for promoting fitness, discipline, and a healthy lifestyle. Off the field, he is involved in charity work and supports various social causes.

Conclusion

Virat Kohli’s journey from a young cricket enthusiast to a global sports icon is a story of passion, dedication, and excellence. He continues to be a role model for aspiring cricketers and sports lovers worldwide.
